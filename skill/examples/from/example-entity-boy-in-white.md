# Boy in White

The **Boy in White** is a mysterious vision in the [[Township]] that traps all those who enter it. He only appears to specific people, and often provides helpful advice for safety.

## Biography

### Background

The Boy in White first appeared to [[Victor]] when he was a child in the 1970s, on the day two new cars arrived on the same day.

As a child, Victor saw the Boy in White talking with [[Christopher]], instructing him to go through the [[Bottle Tree]] in order to free the [[children]]. The Boy explained that the secrets of the [[Township]] lay in its beginnings, with the ritual murder of the children by their loved ones. Before their deaths, another loved one told them a story that gave them hope, and they poured their hope into the roots that became the [[Symbol]], and the roots became the [[Farway Trees]]. Christopher refused to go, but Victor told his mother what he overheard.

The Boy later reappeared to Victor after everyone else had been killed by the [[Creatures]], alongside a [[dog]]. With Victor unable to bury all the bodies, the Boy in white told him to collect precious belongings of the deceased and bury them in a suitcase.

### Season 1

After [[Ethan Matthews]] and his family settle in at [[Colony House]], Ethan sees the Boy in White outside making a shushing gesture. The next morning, Ethan sees the Boy in White outside his window. Ethan later mentions to [[Victor]] that he saw him.

Victor is intrigued, and draws a picture of the Boy in White and asks if it is who Ethan saw. When Ethan confirms that it is, Victor says it means that the Boy was real. Victor and Ethan go in search of the Boy in White in the forest, and he explains to Ethan that it had been so long he thought the Boy in White was a dream, who first appeared after two cars arrived on the same day, just like with Ethan, although long ago. As they talk, Victor reveals that he has been in the Township longer than anyone, but he gets angry and defensive when Ethan asks what happened to his parents. Ethan then reveals that his baby brother, Thomas, died recently. Victor is shocked by this revelation, and Ethan explains that he is telling him because Victor is his friend. Victor then spots the Boy in White and points him out to Ethan, and has them follow.

During the attack on [[Colony House]], Victor helps [[Julie]] escape the house, but they are still in danger of getting caught by the [[Creatures]]. Victor sees the Boy in White, and follows him into the Forest. They reach a [[Farway Tree]], and both of them climb into the hollow.

[[Tabitha]] digs a hole in her basement but the floor collapses and she lands in a cave, where she is found by Victor. Victor says that the Boy in White said she would come, and that he was supposed to wait for her. As tremors shake the house above her, Victor says that it isn’t safe, for the caverns are where the Creatures sleep, and shows her the cave paintings that they make before dragging her off. Meanwhile, [[Boyd]] and [[Sara]] struggle to make their way through the Forest in search of escape or answers. Boyd collapses in pain due to his spider bites and tells Sara to leave him, saying one of them has to make it back. Sara refuses to abandon Boyd. The Boy in White appears and tells her that [[Nathan]] was right and this place and that it is angry now. He tells them to get to safety, and directs her to a Farway Tree. Boyd gets in first, and is transported to the bottom of a dry well.

### Season 2

[[Tabitha]] reaches the [[Lighthouse]] and climbs the steps, hearing a child’s voice say “anghkooey” from up above. She ascends to the top floor and gazes out at the vast expanse. The Boy in White then apologizes, saying this is the only way before pushing her through the window. Tabitha then wakes up in a hospital, seemingly back in the normal world.

### Season 3

After Tabitha sneaks out of [[St. Anthony’s Hospital]], she sees the Boy in White across the street in Camden. Tabitha chases after him and confronts him in a cafe, only to find that it is just a normal boy, whose mother scolds her.

After believing that [[Jasper]] the puppet told [[Christopher]] the story he is trying to remember, Victor eventually realizes that it was the Boy in White talking to Christopher.

[[Victor]] finds the [[Bottle Tree]]/Farway Tree and begins hacking at it with the axe. The Boy in White tells him to stop. Victor replies that the bad tree does bad things. The Boy in White replies that it is important, and Victor frustratedly asks why it is important, and why the Boy will not help them. The Boy replies that he tried to help with [[Christopher]], but now thinks that they need to learn for themselves, as it is the only way they will understand. Victor notices the Boy looks different now, and asks why after years of looking the same. The Boy answers that everything is changing now. Victor begs him to help him understand so he can help his friends, reminding him that they are friends. The Boy simply answers not to cut down the tree and walks off, disappearing into the Forest.

## Trivia

* Although the Boy in White seems to be helping Victor and the others whenever he appears, his appearances also result in negative consequences. Nonetheless, he is, in a way, responsible for the unfolding of events in the series:
  + In "All Good Things...", he guides Julie and Victor to an Farway Tree, saving them from the attack of the Creatures at the Colony House. Victor is transported to the tunnels, being instructed by the Boy in White to stay there waiting for Tabitha. Therefore, in "Strangers in a Strange Land," Tabitha sees the children for the first time. Consequently, these events lead them to the Bottle Tree and, in "Once Upon a Time?", she is brought back to reality in Camden, where she realizes, together with Henry, that Miranda also saw the children. Both are later taken back to the City with Acosta, which may indicate that the boy in white removed Tabitha from that world only so she could understand on her own her purpose in that place in "Revelations - Part 2".
    - This is an indication that the Boy in White can, in some way, control the fate of those who enter an Farway Tree, because Victor is taken to where the Boy wanted (to the tunnels, to guide Tabitha), just as Tabitha is taken to the Lighthouse when entering the Bottle Tree.
      * The fact that the Boy in White makes Tabitha return to the real world so that she can discover on her own her relationship with the ghoulish children is related to what he tells Victor in 'Revelations - Part 1' about them needing to 'learn on their own,' unlike what he did with Christopher.
    - The discovery by Tabitha and Jade leads to Jim's death by the Man in Yellow.
  + In "Oh, the Places We'll Go", the Boy in White appears to save Boyd and Sara and takes them to an Farway Tree, which later leads Boyd to the Dungeon, where Julie saves him in "Thresholds" and causes him to be cursed by the Music Box Curse, which would later mentally lead Julie to this same dungeon - the event that supposedly gave her the ability to travel through time. In this way, by taking Boyd and Sara to the tree, the boy in white directed Boyd to the well, where Julie would throw the rope for him to climb up, being cursed by Martin, causing him to bring the Curse to the town and to Julie herself, who, only because of the Curse, would be able to travel through time to throw the rope to Boyd, creating a temporal paradox. Thus, the boy in white is indirectly responsible for Julie's ability.
