# City and Beach

City and Beach is the first episode of the first segment of Nu Pogodi.

## Plot:

The Wolf is misbehaving in the street: he kicks a trash can twice, causing trash to scatter across the sidewalk, takes a cigarette butt from it and lights it. Then the Wolf notices police bears on a motorcycle, bows to them so that they won't suspect him of smoking and hooliganism, and kicks the trash can again. Soon the water puts out his cigarette, and he notices the Hare on the balcony watering flowers. Seeing a clothesline, the Wolf grabs it and, hooking it onto a television antenna, climbs up, whistling "Song of a Friend" by Vladimir Vysotsky. The Hare cuts the weeds and cuts the rope, and the Wolf falls right into a passing police motorcycle with the phrase: "Well, just you wait-e-e-e-e!"

The Wolf, seeing a trash can in front of him, tries to kick it, as in the pre-story, but notices a sign "To the beach" and goes on. Noticing a poster with a Hare and the slogan "Drink carrot juice!", the Wolf, out of envy, takes a brick, draws a target on the Hare's chest, steps back and shoots without looking. At the moment when he pulls out the trident, the fence falls on him, and he says: "Well, Hare, just you wait!"

After changing his clothes, the Wolf walks along the beach and sees a Hare in front of him, who has decided to go water skiing. At the moment when the Wolf tries to catch his enemy, the boat moves away and drags the Hare along with it. Having missed the moment, the Wolf begins to overtake the boat, lies down on the sand and shoots. The trident pierces the rope, and the Hare falls into the water. Putting on fins and a mask for breathing underwater, the Wolf dives and swims in search of the Hare. On the way, a sparrow lands on his snorkel, and he, suffocating, surfaces and calls for help. Beaver rescuers swim to the Wolf and perform artificial respiration. Seeing the Hare swimming past, the Wolf throws the rescuers into the water, grabs their boat and begins to chase the Hare, but he manages to dive into the water. Having lost the Hare, the Wolf drives out onto the beach, rams the fence and drives out onto the highway, where cars are driving past.

Reversing back to the beach, the Wolf sees the Hare, climbs a tree, prepares a rope and lowers it down to the Hare in a loop. The Hare shoots cherry pits upward, but when he shoots a peach pit, the Wolf gets caught in the loop with which he wanted to catch the Hare. Soon the Hare leaves, and a family of hedgehogs lie down in his place. Unable to bear the weight, the branch with the Wolf breaks, and he falls right on the hedgehogs. Howling and crying in pain, the Wolf again sees the Hare, who is climbing up the diving board. The Wolf does not have time to catch the Hare, because he pushes off the board and jumps into the water. The board catapults the Wolf, and he, having flown over the beach, lands next to the Pig, who inflates a rubber hare and throws it next to the Wolf. Having woken up, the Wolf sees a toy Pig near him, then, mistaking it for a real Hare, he swings and gobbles it up. Out of fear, the Wolf runs away and again sees a real Hare in front of him, who has once again decided to go water skiing. The Hare throws a water skier's tow rope over the Wolf, and the boat pulls him along. Rushing across the water, the Wolf shouts to the Hare from afar: "Well, just you wait!"
