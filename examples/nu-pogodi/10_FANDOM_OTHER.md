## Pan Am 103


Source: https://nu-pogodi.fandom.com/wiki/Pan_Am_103


# Pan Am 103

**Pan Am Flight 103** (**PA103/PAA103**) was a regularly scheduled Pan Am transatlantic flight from Frankfurt to Detroit via a stopover in London and another in New York City. Shortly after 19:00 on 21 December 1988, the Boeing 747 "Clipper Maid of the Seas" was destroyed by a bomb while flying over the Scottish town of Lockerbie, killing all 243 passengers and 16 crew aboard. Large sections of the aircraft crashed in a residential street in Lockerbie, killing 11 residents. With a total of 270 fatalities, the event, which became known as the **Lockerbie bombing**, is the deadliest terrorist attack in the history of the United Kingdom.

Following a three-year joint investigation by Dumfries and Galloway Constabulary and the US Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), arrest warrants were issued for two Libyan nationals in 1991. After protracted negotiations and United Nations sanctions, in 1999, Libyan leader Muammar Gaddafi handed over the two men for trial at Camp Zeist, the Netherlands. In 2001, Abdelbaset al-Megrahi, a Libyan intelligence officer, was found guilty of 270 counts of murder in connection with the bombing, and was sentenced to life imprisonment. His co-accused, Lamin Khalifah Fhimah, was acquitted. In 2009, Megrahi was released by the Scottish Government on compassionate grounds after being diagnosed with prostate cancer. He died in 2012 as the only person to be convicted for the attack.

In 2003, Gaddafi accepted Libya's responsibility for the Lockerbie bombing, and paid more than US$1 billion in compensation to the families of the victims. Although Gaddafi maintained that he had never personally given the order for the attack, acceptance of Megrahi's status as a government employee was used to connect responsibility by Libya with a series of requirements laid out by a UN resolution for sanctions against Libya to be lifted. In 2011, during the First Libyan Civil War, former Minister of Justice Mustafa Abdul Jalil said that Gaddafi personally ordered the bombing.

As all the accomplices required for such a complex operation were never identified, or convicted, many conspiracy theories have swirled, such as East German Stasi agents having a possible role in the attack. Some relatives of the dead, including Lockerbie campaigner Jim Swire, believe the bomb was planted at Heathrow Airport, possibly by a sleeper cell belonging to the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine – General Command, which had been operating in West Germany in the months before the Pan Am bombing, and not sent via feeder flights from Malta, as suggested by the US and UK governments.

In 2020, US authorities indicted the Tunisian resident and Libyan national Abu Agila Masud, who was 37 years old at the time of the incident, for participating in the bombing. He was taken into custody in 2022, pleading not guilty in 2023. A federal trial is set for 2026.

Pan Am 103 was the second Boeing 747 which was lost to a mid-air bombing, after Air India 182 in June 1985.

## Aircraft:

The aircraft operating Pan Am Flight 103 was a Boeing 747-121, MSN 19646, registered as N739PA and named *Clipper Maid of the Seas.* Before 1979, it had been named *Clipper Morning Light*.[*citation needed*] It was the 15th 747 built and had first flown on 25 January 1970. It was delivered to Pan Am on 15 February, one month after the first 747 entered service with Pan Am. In 1978, as *Clipper Morning Light*, it had appeared in "Conquering the Atlantic", the fourth episode of the BBC Television documentary series *Diamonds in the Sky*, presented by Julian Pettifer.

## Investigation:

The original prime suspect in the bombing was the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine – General Command (PFLP-GC), a Syria-based group led by Ahmed Jibril. # Soyuzmultfilm

**Soyuzmultfilm** (Russian: **Союзмультфи́льм**, lit. **Unioncartoon**), also known in English as **SMF Animation Studio** and formerly known as **Soyuzdetmultfilm** (**Союздетмультфи́льм**, **Unionchildcartoon**), is a Russian animation studio, production, and distribution company based in Moscow. Launched on June 10, 1936 as the animated film production unit of the U.S.S.R.'s motion picture monopoly, GUKF, Soyuzmultfilm has produced more than 1,500 cartoons.

# Well, Just You Wait!

**Well, Just You Wait!** (Russian: **Ну, погоди!**, romanized: **Nu, pogodi!**; also known as **You Just Wait!** in the English dub and **I'll Get You!** in official translations) is a Soviet and Russian series of animated short films produced by [Soyuzmultfilm](/wiki/Soyuzmultfilm "Soyuzmultfilm").

## History

In 1968, the director of the Soyuzmultfilm film studio, Mikhail Valkov, asked young humorist writers Alexander Kurlyandsky, Felix Kamov, Arkady Khait and Eduard Uspensky to come up with a funny script for a cartoon for children.

In 1969, Kurlyandsky and Khait came up with a plot. They decided that it should be a chase film. They chose who would be chasing whom. They went through different characters: at first they wanted to take a fox and a rooster, then a fox and a hare, and finally settled on a wolf and a hare, as the most familiar characters in Russian folklore.

The team of scriptwriters had to implement the idea in one of the stories of the 1st issue of the film magazine "Happy Merry-Go-Round". In the course of thinking about the Wolf's threatening phrase, which was supposed to become a link between two different episodes, the phrase "Well, just you wait!" was chosen. The phrase was originally uttered in the 1961 Soviet film *Girls* and the 1965 stop-motion animated film *Whose Cones in the Forest?*

Vyacheslav Kotyonochkin's longtime collaborator, Svetozar Rusakov, served as production designer. It's worth noting that in 1967, two years before the release of *Happy Merry-Go-Round* #1, Kotyonochkin and Rusakov created the short animated film *Hare's Horns* for the all-Union satirical film magazine [Fitil](/wiki/Fitil "Fitil") (issue #61), in which the cartoon images of the Hare and the Wolf are similar in appearance to those that would later appear in *Well, Just You Wait!*

## English versions

According to *The Gazette of India*, the first English dub was produced between 1970s and 1980s for Sovexportfilm (now Roskino) in India.[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-2) This dub is completely lost.

In the 1980s, Filmexport Studios produced the second English dub of the series again by Sovexportfilm. Hare's name was changed to Rabbit. The English credit translations were done by Nikolai Kurnakov and Evelyn Matusevich, while the voice actors were uncredited. It is unknown what year episode 12 was dubbed, but episode 15 is credited to have been dubbed in 1986, and episodes 1-11, 13, 14, and 16 were dubbed in 1987. This dub was released on VHS by Sefa Video in Australia (it is unknown if the tape will be scanned and posted online) and seven episodes were also released by ComicT
